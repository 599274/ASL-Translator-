About Our Project

Our Project known as the ASL Translator, is able to recognize various ASL letters from the alphabet through machine learning, allowing us to translate its outputs into English.

Introduction

Our Project known as the ASL Translator, is able to recognize various ASL letters from the alphabet through machine learning, allowing us to translate its outputs into English.

Motivation

The ASL Translator uses YOLOV5, a neural network which can detect objects either through photos or real time camera feeds. With this, we trained our model to recognize All letters in the ASL Alphabet so it can detect them in the real world.

The process

  • 01Deciding on the Project

    Before starting the project, we had to outline what our model should do. After an unsuccessful attempt at a different project, an algorithm that could read and play sheet music, we decided on the ASL translator as a group. This project has a real life application; mute people who can now communicate with people who don't know ASL simpler.

  • 02Gathering the Data

    Gathering the data is a step that is vital for a good algorithm. No matter how good the model you are training, if the data is faulty, none of the algoritms predictions are going to be good. Luckily for us, somebody else had already made a dataset with around 1,500 images, to which we added roughly 1,000 more of our own images. Using images that we took in front of our faces trained our model to distinguish between a face and a hand.

  • 03Training the Model

    Training the model is one of the last steps before implementation. Instead of training the model on the 2,500 images we had, we added another step called data augmentation. This step takes the data and slightly modifies it, like adding contrast or noise (random pixels thrown into the image). Data augmentation brought our final data count up to 5,000, quality and quantity of data.

  • 04Creating our website

    Once we had a trained model with all of its features, we needed a website that showcased the AI and explained everything about it. We uploaded a template and dowloaded it CoCalc- a platform that allowed collaborative work on the site. We used HTML to edit the writing and put the information down. Then we made use of CSS for colors, speed, format, etc. The final product is exactly what you are reading! (how did we do?)
